## Long-term direction

Toward modern arithmetic geometry and number theory — eventually
Langlands-area papers. Rough arc: commutative algebra → algebraic
geometry (schemes, cohomology) → algebraic number theory → class
field theory → modular forms → Galois representations. Learning for
fun; happy to take detours.

Parallel track: differential geometry. A lot of the intuition behind
algebraic geometry (tangent spaces, bundles, forms, cohomology) seems
to come from the differential side first, so I want a path through
manifolds, differential forms, connections, Riemannian geometry —
mostly as an intuition pump rather than an end in itself.

## Taste

Prefer:
- Motivated narrative — author explains *why* before using.
- Heavy exercise sets (every exercise goes into Lean).
- Concrete examples before abstract theorems.
- Designed for cover-to-cover, not reference.

Avoid:
- Terse theorem–proof without motivation (Bourbaki-adjacent).
- Encyclopedic references (Lang, Dummit–Foote, Knapp) — use Mathlib for lookup.
- Books that skip examples for generality.
- Light or absent exercises.

Good fit: Tao *Analysis*, Axler, Vakil *Rising Sea*, Silverman *Elliptic Curves*.
Poor fit (kept if nothing better): Atiyah–Macdonald, Hartshorne, Lang *Algebra*.

## Pacing

~5–10 hrs/week sustained. Books that take 3–12 months are fine. Lean
companion adds 2–3× to calendar time.
